## Context

Sahl Hasheesh is a self-contained resort corridor south of central Hurghada with high German guest density. The driving distance back to the city is operationally significant for an unwell traveler, so three on-zone locations remove the need to leave the corridor for routine outpatient care.

## Key points

- **Three on-zone clinics.** Sahl Hasheesh Medical Center, a second outpatient site, and a hotel-zone clinic together cover the corridor.
- **Main outpatient and observation capability.** The principal site carries full outpatient scope including observation cases that would otherwise be referred north.
- **Pediatric coverage on-zone.** A dedicated pediatric flow handles the high share of family travelers in the resort cluster.
- **Resort-gate access agreements.** Standing agreements with the major hotel operators allow direct entry for HMC physicians and nurses.
- **Mobile dispatch from within the corridor.** Hotel-room visits are dispatched from on-zone, not from Hurghada, keeping arrival times short.

## What this means for ADAC

For travelers staying in Sahl Hasheesh, the first medical contact stays inside the resort corridor. This removes the transport friction that otherwise extends case duration and avoids unnecessary referrals back into Hurghada for cases that can be managed locally.
