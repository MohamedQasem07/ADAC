## Context

A defined set of items sits outside the flat rate. These are always quoted before any work begins and appear as distinct lines on the invoice rather than being absorbed silently. The list is intentionally short, so that the line between "inside the package" and "quoted separately" remains easy to apply at the bedside.

## Key points

- **Clinical escalation.** Ambulance dispatch, ER coordination, and inpatient admission are quoted separately under the escalation pathway.
- **Advanced imaging and labs.** Imaging or laboratory work beyond the scope of the originating package is quoted on request, with pre-authorization required.
- **Specialty consults.** Specialist input outside the standard outpatient scope, such as cardiology workup beyond the package, is quoted separately.
- **Third-party logistics.** Transport, repatriation coordination, or other third-party services are quoted at cost and clearly marked on the invoice.
- **Non-catalogue procedures.** Anything not listed in the published catalogue is quoted on request and requires pre-authorization before work begins.

## What this means for ADAC

The boundary between flat-rate work and separately-quoted work is published rather than discretionary. ADAC sees a quote before any out-of-scope work starts, and the invoice carries those items as distinct lines, which keeps escalation costs visible rather than blended into a higher number.
