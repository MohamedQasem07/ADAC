## Context

During an escalation, uncertainty is operationally expensive on both sides. Communication with ADAC is therefore structured and time-bound rather than ad-hoc. Every escalation is recorded as a distinct event in the case record so it can be reviewed independently of the underlying clinical case.

## Key points

- **Immediate notification at decision.** ADAC is informed at the moment the treating physician calls the escalation, before transport even leaves.
- **Status update at admission.** A second update is sent when the traveler is accepted by the receiving facility, with the admitting team and bed type recorded.
- **Written escalation summary.** A full written summary is dispatched within the target window after case closure, including the clinical reason for escalation and the outcome.
- **Case coordinator stays on the line.** A single coordinator owns the communication channel from escalation decision through clinical handover.
- **Escalation logged as a distinct event.** The escalation is recorded separately in the case file, with its own timestamps and documentation set.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC sees the escalation in real time rather than learning about it after the fact. The chain of notifications is documented, the coordinator is identifiable, and the escalation can be reviewed as a discrete event during operational review rather than reconstructed from the broader case narrative.
