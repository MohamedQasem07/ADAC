## Context

The receiving emergency department gets a structured clinical handover before the traveler arrives, so triage at the door is not starting from zero. The objective is to preserve clinical continuity across the transition and to record the moment that responsibility moves from the HMC physician to the ER team.

## Key points

- **Written clinical handover.** A structured document accompanies the patient, capturing presenting complaint, working impression, and any procedures already performed.
- **Vital signs at transfer.** The last full set of vitals before handover is recorded on the handover document.
- **Medications administered.** Every drug given during the outpatient phase is listed with dose, route, and time.
- **Preliminary ICD-10.** The treating physician records a preliminary diagnostic code, which the ER team confirms or revises on arrival.
- **Named ER physician contact.** A specific ER physician is identified as the receiving contact, with phone reachable through the case coordinator until clinical responsibility transfers.

## What this means for ADAC

ER arrival is a continuation of an already-documented case rather than a fresh presentation. The handover record is auditable, the moment of clinical responsibility transfer is explicit, and ADAC's case coordinator has a single named contact on the receiving side.
