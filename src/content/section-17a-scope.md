## Question

Which outpatient scenarios should be in the pilot, and which candidate families do we activate for the 1 July start?

## HMC suggested position

- **Nine active categories as the baseline.** The current 9 categories cover the common cases ADAC travelers present with along the Red Sea corridor.
- **GI, RX, WD, OR carry the heaviest weight.** Per §3 data, these four families account for the bulk of observed case volume and should be confirmed first.
- **Seven candidate families ready for June alignment.** UTI, migraine, cellulitis, lab add-on, imaging add-on, marine sting, and vertigo are scoped and ready for activation on 1 July.
- **Out-of-scope cases follow §11 escalation.** Anything outside the agreed scope routes through the documented escalation pathway with no exceptions.

## What we need ADAC to confirm

Confirm the agreed pilot scope list and indicate which candidate families to activate by 1 July, so June preparation can lock the operational checklists.

## Risk / note

Mid-pilot scope expansion is possible but the §11 escalation pathway stays unchanged for any out-of-scope case that presents in the interim.
