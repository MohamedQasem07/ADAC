## Context

HMC is staffed by a permanent multidisciplinary team that operates around the clock, not a thin roster topped up by freelancers. The team is organized into three layers — clinical, operations and reporting — so that an ADAC case has a defined owner at every step from first call to final documentation.

## Key points

- **Senior physicians on duty 24/7.** General medicine, emergency medicine, surgery, paediatrics, cardiology and ENT are all represented on the active duty roster across the network.
- **Nursing and paramedic backbone.** Trained nursing and emergency paramedic teams cover clinic and on-site visits, including hotel-room and mobile-unit care.
- **Dedicated Operations Department.** Case coordinators handle assistance-partner intake, triage routing, escalation flagging and same-day case-summary turnaround.
- **German-speaking coverage where possible.** A subset of physicians and coordinators communicate in German; English is the operational default, with Arabic and Russian for local logistics.
- **Reporting discipline.** A reporting layer produces same-day case summaries and monthly performance reports for assistance partners — not generated ad hoc.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC always has a named operational counterpart at HMC. Cases are not bounced between freelancers; they sit inside a defined clinical-operations-reporting chain with documented handovers.
