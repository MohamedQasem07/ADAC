## Context

Itemized billing in tourist-medical settings is the single largest source of post-case variance for an assistance company. Consumables, observation time, and time-of-day surcharges compound unpredictably across a season. The flat-rate framework removes this category of variance at the source — ADAC sees one number per clinical presentation, locked before treatment begins.

## Key points

- **One number per package.** Each of the 65 packages across 9 categories carries a single flat rate, agreed at pre-authorization.
- **No itemized surprises.** Consumables, observation hours, and out-of-hours premiums are absorbed inside the package rate, not invoiced separately.
- **Reserve setting becomes mechanical.** ADAC books a reserve from the published catalogue at notification, not from a downstream estimate.
- **Variance moves up one level.** Forecasting shifts from invoice-line variance to case-mix variance, which is far easier to model across a season.
- **Out-of-scope items are explicit.** Anything outside the package is pre-quoted separately under §13.3, never folded silently into a final bill.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC can budget the Red Sea corridor at portfolio level rather than case level. Per-case cost is a known input at the moment a case opens, which removes the dominant source of late-cycle financial surprise across the AG Holder book.
