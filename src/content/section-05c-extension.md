## Context

The northern and southern extremes of the Red Sea tourist corridor sit outside the urban core but carry meaningful German traveler volume, particularly in the dive-resort segment. Coverage combines a fixed anchor location with on-call physician dispatch, semi-ambulance transport, and a defined remote-triage protocol.

## Key points

- **Soma Bay anchor location.** A fixed clinic anchors Safaga and the northern resort zone, with full outpatient scope on site.
- **Marsa Alam covered by extension.** On-call physician dispatch, semi-ambulance transport, and remote triage cover the southern corridor.
- **Mobile units bridge driving distance.** Mobile clinic deployment shortens the effective response time across the longer distances of the extended zone.
- **Hotel and dive-resort visits in scope.** Room visits and dive-resort attendance are part of the standard extension protocol.
- **Pre-defined escalation routes.** Imaging-required and extended-observation cases are routed to the nearest equipped site under a documented protocol.

## What this means for ADAC

Extension coverage gives ADAC operational reach into resort areas that otherwise sit outside any single fixed footprint. The framework targets the same scope and the same package pricing as the urban core, so case handling does not change based on where in the corridor the traveler is staying.
