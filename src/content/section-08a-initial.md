## Context

Initial assessment is the first clinical contact after ADAC notifies HMC. A physician reviews presenting symptoms, vitals, and history against a fixed intake checklist. The output of this step determines whether the case fits the package framework or requires an escalation pathway, so it is treated as a clinical step rather than an administrative one.

## Key points

- **Physician-led intake.** A licensed physician — not a call-center agent — captures the presenting complaint, duration, and severity in structured form.
- **Vitals and red flags.** Blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation, and obvious red flags such as chest pain or hemodynamic instability are recorded at first contact.
- **Traveler context.** Age, key medical history, current medications, allergies, hotel location, and mobility status are logged before the case is classified.
- **Language and companion.** Preferred language and presence of a companion are noted so the right physician and coordinator are assigned.
- **Single intake record.** All inputs land in one case record that follows the traveler through triage, treatment, and reporting.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC receives a clinically grounded first read within the response-time target, not a triage based on form fields alone. The same record drives package selection, dispatch, and the closing report, which means fewer handoffs and a cleaner audit trail per case.
