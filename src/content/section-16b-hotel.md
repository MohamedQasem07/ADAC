## Context

A traveler who is feverish, dehydrated, or unsteady should not be moved unless clinically necessary. The HMC operating model treats the hotel room as a valid clinical setting for a defined range of cases, with the same physician-and-nurse team and the same package price as a clinic visit. Mode of delivery does not change the rate.

## Key points

- **Physician and nurse, on site.** A two-person clinical team arrives at the room with full portable equipment, not a paramedic alone.
- **Most workups bedside.** IV therapy, ECG, wound care, suturing, and portable X-Ray are completable in the room for the relevant packages.
- **Same price, same scope.** The package rate is identical whether delivery is at the clinic or at the room — mode does not alter the flat rate.
- **74% Normal Room baseline.** Across the documented case mix, the majority of admissions resolve at standard room level, which maps well to hotel-room delivery for ambulatory presentations.
- **Privacy of the room.** The traveler avoids a clinic waiting area, which matters for fever, gastrointestinal cases, and elderly travelers.

## What this means for AG Holders

When leaving the room is the wrong clinical decision, leaving the room stops being required. The traveler receives the same standard of care, at the same cost to ADAC, in a setting that is materially more comfortable than a clinic chair.
