## Context

The monthly report consolidates the case-level documentation into a single operational view. It is sent as a standing deliverable, not as something ADAC has to request, so the operational picture is visible by default. The intent is to surface trends early enough to act on them rather than to compile evidence after the fact.

## Key points

- **Case volume and severity mix.** Total cases, distribution across the nine clinical categories, and the proportion handled at each severity tier.
- **Escalation rate.** Share of cases that crossed into the ambulance or ER pathway, with the clinical reason recorded for each.
- **Same-day closure rate.** Proportion of cases where the clinical summary and invoice were dispatched on the day of case closure.
- **Average response time.** Time from initial contact to physician on scene, reported as a distribution rather than a single average.
- **Language and documentation coverage.** Share of cases handled in German, and completeness rate of the structured documentation set.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC sees the operational and clinical picture without having to ask. Trends in escalation, response time, or documentation quality become visible early, and the monthly cadence creates a fixed point for joint operational review rather than ad-hoc clarifications.
