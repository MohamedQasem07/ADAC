## Context

Once a red flag is identified, ambulance dispatch runs on a defined channel rather than an open phone tree. The intent is to compress the interval between clinical decision and patient movement, and to make sure the receiving hospital is already preparing while transport is in motion.

## Key points

- **Direct dispatch link.** A radio and phone link into the ambulance service is opened by the treating physician at the moment of escalation.
- **Capability matched to case.** Full ambulance with paramedic capability is dispatched for unstable patients; semi-ambulance transport is used for stable transfers requiring imaging or admission.
- **ETA target windows.** Dispatch and arrival targets are tracked per case and reported in the monthly performance report.
- **Named receiving hospital.** The transfer destination is named at the point of dispatch, not selected en route.
- **Stabilization continues in transit.** Clinical treatment does not pause for transport; the bedside team hands over to the ambulance crew without a break in care.

## What this means for ADAC

Transport is a continuation of treatment rather than a logistical handover. ADAC has a single, documented chain from red flag to hospital door, and the ETA and receiving facility are known and recorded at the moment escalation is called.
