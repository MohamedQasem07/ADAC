## Context

When a traveler falls ill in a resort zone, the practical question is not which clinic is best in theory but which clinic is reachable now. The HMC network is built around where AG Holders actually stay — eleven clinical locations along the Red Sea corridor, all operating around the clock, with hotel-room visits available where leaving the room is not realistic.

## Key points

- **Eleven clinics along the corridor.** Hurghada, Sahl Hasheesh, Safaga, Soma Bay and Marsa Alam are covered under one network.
- **24/7 at every location.** Cover does not narrow at night or on weekends — the operational standard is the same across all hours.
- **Walk-in or call from the room.** AG Holders can present at a clinic or call the Operations Department from the hotel; both routes reach the same network.
- **Hotel-room visits as default option.** For green and yellow cases, the team comes to the traveler rather than requiring transport to a clinic.
- **One hotline regardless of route.** Whether the traveler is in Hurghada or Marsa Alam, the entry point is one number to the Operations Department.

## What this means for AG Holders

Access does not depend on knowing the geography of the Red Sea. From any hotel along the corridor, one call reaches a clinical team that arrives quickly and works to the same standard — the traveler does not have to make routing decisions in a moment of stress.
