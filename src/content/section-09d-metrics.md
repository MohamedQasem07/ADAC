## Context

Quality metrics are the data that sits underneath every reporting cadence. The same set of measures is reported every month so trend lines stay legible across the year. Definitions are written once and held fixed — metrics are reported, not negotiated.

## Key points

- **Case volume and mix.** Total cases, count by category across the 9 categories, and distribution across the 65 packages.
- **Severity and escalation.** Green / Yellow / Red mix per period, escalation rate, and time from triage decision to ambulance dispatch where relevant.
- **Response-time hit-rates.** Percentage of cases meeting acknowledgement, triage, and dispatch targets, with same-day-closure rate tracked against the 83% within 48h benchmark.
- **Operational quality.** Average time to first physician contact, language coverage delivered versus requested, and documentation completeness on closed cases.
- **Traveler feedback.** Anonymized feedback summary where collected, separated from clinical metrics so the two are not conflated.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC reads the same dashboard every month with no shifting definitions. Metrics are reported as observed rather than tuned to look favorable, which means the medical-assistance team can spot drift early and raise it inside the standard cadence rather than through escalation.
