## Context

Trust in a tourist-medical provider cannot be asserted on a slide — it accumulates across many cases handled to the same standard, and it is visible on platforms HMC does not control. The AG Holder benefits from a reputation that is independently verifiable, not from a curated set of testimonials.

## Key points

- **Same standard for every traveler.** AG Holders receive the same Detailed Medical Report format and the same flat-rate billing applied to every other patient at HMC.
- **No surprise invoices.** The flat-rate framework removes the largest single source of post-case complaint in tourist-medical settings.
- **Independently verifiable reviews.** HMC's public presence on Google, TripAdvisor, and Trustpilot is open to the AG Holder before, during, and after a case — no curation, no marketing testimonials.
- **Long-standing presence in the corridor.** HMC's reputation in the Hurghada tourist-medical sector has been built over years of consistent operations, not a recent rebrand.
- **Feedback reported back to ADAC.** Post-case AG Holder feedback is collected anonymously and summarized to ADAC monthly, so the trust signal flows to ADAC as well.

## What this means for AG Holders

The traveler can verify the provider on the same platforms they use for hotels and flights, before they ever need care. The trust signal is external, durable, and consistent with what they will actually experience on the ground.
