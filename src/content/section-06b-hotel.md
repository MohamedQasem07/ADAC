## Context

Hotel Room Visit brings the clinic to the traveler when transport is impractical, undesirable or clinically inappropriate. An HMC physician and nurse arrive at the hotel room with a portable diagnostic kit and complete the same intake, examination and treatment workflow used in a clinic. This mode is critical for GI cases with mild dehydration, respiratory complaints, wound care and after-hours scenarios where moving the traveler creates more risk than value.

## Key points

- **Physician-led on-site team.** A doctor plus nurse attend together, not a paramedic alone, so the clinical decision is made at the bedside.
- **Portable diagnostic kit.** Vitals, ECG, SpO2, nebulizer, IV station, wound-care field kit and portable X-Ray where indicated for trauma triage.
- **Identical package price.** Mode of delivery does not change pricing — hotel cases settle on the same package code as a clinic case.
- **German-speaking physician available on request.** Language alignment is treated as part of clinical quality, not an upsell.
- **Same documentation standard.** Structured medical report drafted at the bedside and completed same day.

## What this means for ADAC

Hotel Room Visit removes the cost and friction of transport while preserving the clinical and documentation standard ADAC expects. For travelers, it lowers the threshold to seek care early, which tends to shorten the case and reduce the chance of escalation — a cleaner claims cycle for the same package price.
