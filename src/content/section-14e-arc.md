The Red Sea outpatient package model is being built first with ADAC. Once the pilot is documented and governed, HMC aims to extend the conversation to the broader European assistance ecosystem that ADAC sits within — beginning with ARC Europe.

## Strategic next step

- HMC aims to arrange a follow-up meeting in June with ARC Europe at their headquarters.
- The objective would be to explore how the outpatient package framework could be presented to ARC Europe and, where appropriate, extended to relevant affiliated mobility and assistance partners.
- This would support a scalable, documented, and standardized outpatient care model for travelers across the Red Sea region.

## Why ARC Europe matters

- ARC Europe is a pan-European mobility and assistance network.
- It is associated with major European automobile clubs and assistance partners.
- Its network is relevant because ADAC is part of the European automobile club ecosystem.

## What HMC would like to discuss

- Introduce the Red Sea outpatient package model.
- Discuss pilot package governance and documentation.
- Explore whether the model could be relevant to other ARC Europe-related partners.
- Agree on a scalable cooperation pathway after the ADAC pilot.

## Desired outcome

A constructive, exploratory exchange — not a commitment. The follow-up meeting would aim to test whether the outpatient package model can serve as a reference architecture for a wider European partner conversation, with the ADAC pilot acting as the proof point. No formal partnership, approval, or rollout beyond the ADAC scope is implied at this stage.
