## Context

A flat-rate package replaces a multi-line invoice with a single figure tied to a clinical presentation. The scope is published in advance, so approval becomes a yes or no decision against a catalogue rather than a negotiation about individual items. Escalation is priced separately and only when clinically required.

## Key points

- **One number per package.** Each of the 65 packages across the 9 categories carries a fixed price covering the defined scope.
- **No surprise line items.** Consumables, observation time, and routine ancillaries are inside the package price, not added afterward.
- **Escalation is quoted separately.** When a case needs inpatient referral or imaging beyond scope, that decision is documented and priced on its own.
- **Delivery mode does not change the price.** Clinic, hotel-room, and mobile delivery of the same package carry the same figure.
- **Invoice maps to catalogue code.** Reconciliation is a one-to-one match between the package code and the approved scope.

## What this means for ADAC

Flat rates convert the claims cycle from line-item review to package confirmation. Approval is faster, disputes are concentrated on the few cases that legitimately escalate, and the desk closes routine outpatient cases in hours rather than weeks. The framework targets a measurable reduction in reconciliation work on both sides.
