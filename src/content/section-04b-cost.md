## Context

Itemized billing is the default in most outpatient settings, but it is poorly matched to how an assistance company actually closes a case. Every consumable, every observation hour, and every ancillary test is judged on its own line. A clinically routine case can stay open while a single item is re-priced or queried.

## Key points

- **Line-item disputes dominate cycle time.** Reconciliation effort concentrates on consumables and observation time rather than the clinical decision itself.
- **Pre-authorization is slow without a fixed number.** Approval is held back by uncertainty about what the final bill will look like.
- **Active caseload grows mechanically.** Cases that should close same-week stay open, raising the headcount cost of running the desk.
- **Travelers receive unclear answers.** When the price is not fixed, neither the clinic nor the assistance desk can confirm coverage cleanly at the bedside.
- **Audit work persists after closure.** Even closed cases attract retrospective queries when the underlying invoice is line-item based.

## What this means for ADAC

Cost predictability is not a procurement question — it is a claims-cycle question. A fixed price per clinical presentation collapses the dispute surface to a single figure, shortens approval, and frees desk capacity for the cases that genuinely require attention.
