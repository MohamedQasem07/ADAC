## Context

Documentation is the artifact ADAC and the traveler take away from the case. A structured medical report is started at the bedside by the treating physician and finalized the same day, using a fixed template that maps to the package code, the ICD-10 diagnosis, the procedures performed and the medications given. This is the document that drives reimbursement, downstream care in Germany and any follow-up assessment.

## Key points

- **Drafted at the point of care.** The treating physician writes the report, not a back-office transcriber working from fragments.
- **Structured template.** Diagnosis (ICD-10), procedures, medications, physician notes, disposition and safety-net advice in a fixed order.
- **Same-day completion target.** The report is dispatched to ADAC by the agreed channel before the end of the operational day.
- **Package code on the report.** The assigned package is on the document, so the medical report and the invoice reconcile on a single key.
- **English by default, German on request.** Language is matched to the recipient without changing the underlying structure.

## What this means for ADAC

A medical report that is structured, timely and reconcilable against the invoice is the difference between a clean claim and a back-and-forth. Same-day dispatch shortens the closure window, and the fixed template means ADAC's assessors are reading the same document layout every time, regardless of who treated the case.
