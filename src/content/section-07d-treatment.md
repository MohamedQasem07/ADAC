## Context

Treatment is the clinical core of the journey. The physician and nurse arrive at the agreed location with the kit matched to the assigned package, complete a structured intake, run the diagnostics defined in the package, and deliver therapy to the published scope. Clinical notes are captured in real time so documentation does not lag the bedside. If clinical reality moves beyond what the package covers, the case is escalated immediately rather than quietly stretched.

## Key points

- **Standard intake every time.** Vital signs, focused history and examination open every case, regardless of delivery mode.
- **Package-matched diagnostics and therapy.** IV, medication, imaging, suturing and other interventions are delivered against the published package scope.
- **Real-time clinical notes.** Documentation is captured during treatment, not reconstructed afterwards, which protects the medical report.
- **Scope deviations flagged immediately.** Anything that exceeds the assigned package is escalated to the case coordinator before it appears in the invoice.
- **Same clinical standard across all three modes.** Clinic, hotel and mobile-unit cases follow the same workflow and the same evidence base.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC gets clinical work that matches the package that was authorized — no quiet scope creep, no end-of-case surprises. When a case genuinely needs more than the package covers, escalation is visible in writing as it happens, which protects both the traveler's clinical outcome and ADAC's reimbursement position.
