---
title: "Reduced Disputes"
eyebrow: "§15.3"
summary: "Disputes drop when the cost has been agreed before treatment began."
layout: "editorial"
---

A flat-rate framework removes the single biggest driver of disputes: post-treatment surprise at the invoice. Both teams discuss scope, not price, once treatment is underway.

- Pre-authorization is yes/no on a published number — no quantity-of-consumables negotiation
- No "extras" lines on invoices, by design
- Out-of-scope items (§13.3) are explicit and pre-quoted
- Dispute energy shifts from per-line to overall scope-fit, which is rarer
- Joint operational dashboard shows escalations, not micro-line-items
