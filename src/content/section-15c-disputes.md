## Context

The most common dispute category in tourist-medical claims is line-item disagreement: which consumable, how many units, how many observation hours. A flat-rate framework removes this category by design — there are no lines to dispute, only one agreed package price per clinical presentation.

## Key points

- **Price agreed pre-treatment.** Pre-authorization is a yes/no on a published number, not a quantity negotiation after the fact.
- **No extras lines.** Consumables, observation, and routine workup are inside the package — there is no separate invoicing channel for them.
- **Escalation is clearly marked.** When a case clinically escalates beyond the package, the escalation is documented and quoted separately under §13.3, never blended in.
- **Scope-fit, not line-items.** Any remaining dispute concerns whether the right package was chosen — a single, resolvable question rather than a list of micro-disagreements.
- **Joint dashboard reports escalations.** Operational reporting surfaces escalations and scope queries to ADAC monthly, not raw line-level data.

## What this means for ADAC

Dispute volume per case drops sharply, and the disputes that remain are clinical-scope questions answerable from the medical report. ADAC's case-handling effort per file falls, and the AG Holder is not caught between two parties negotiating an invoice after they have already gone home.
