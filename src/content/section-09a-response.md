## Context

Response times are operational targets, not legally binding guarantees. They cover the window from ADAC notification through acknowledgement, triage decision, physician dispatch, and case closure. Each target is measured per case, aggregated monthly, and reported back to ADAC in the standard performance summary.

## Key points

- **Acknowledgement target.** Within 15 minutes of ADAC notification, the case is acknowledged and a coordinator is named.
- **Triage decision target.** Within 30 minutes, the severity band is set and the package proposal or escalation decision is communicated.
- **Dispatch target.** Physician dispatch for hotel-room visits within 45 minutes in central zones, with longer windows recorded transparently for remote locations.
- **Escalation notification target.** Red-flag cases trigger ADAC notification within 15 minutes of the escalation decision.
- **Same-day closure target.** The detailed medical report and package-coded invoice issue on the same operational day the case closes, in line with the 83% within 48h benchmark.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC has a clear, written set of operational targets to measure HMC against. Hit-rates are reported monthly rather than self-declared, and the targets cover both routine cases and escalations, which gives the assistance team the same visibility for green cases and red ones.
