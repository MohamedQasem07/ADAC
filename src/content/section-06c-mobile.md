## Context

The Mobile Clinic Unit is an equipped clinical vehicle deployed when neither Clinic Visit nor Hotel Room Visit fits — cases that can't reach a clinic, need extended observation or involve several travelers at once. It is the operational answer to the Soma Bay / Marsa Alam geography, where distance between hotel clusters and fixed clinics can be material. The unit is typically parked at the hotel gate or in a resort common area with management coordination.

## Key points

- **Full IV and observation capacity.** IV therapy station, observation seating, ECG and portable X-Ray inside the vehicle.
- **Multi-patient capability.** Designed for resort outbreak scenarios — food-borne GI clusters, allergic clusters — where several AG Holders may need care in parallel.
- **Geographic reach.** Bridges the Soma Bay and Marsa Alam corridor where fixed-clinic transit time would otherwise drive cases toward unnecessary escalation.
- **Same package price.** Mode of delivery does not change pricing — the unit settles on the same package code as a clinic or hotel case.
- **Resort coordination built in.** Deployment is coordinated with hotel management so reception and parking are pre-arranged.

## What this means for ADAC

The Mobile Clinic Unit closes the gap between hotel-room limits and clinic transport, giving ADAC a third operational lever for cases that would otherwise be forced into escalation. Predictable price, predictable documentation, and a real answer for outbreak-style events where speed of access matters more than the location of the care.
