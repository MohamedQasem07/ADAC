## Context

Hurghada concentrates the majority of HMC outpatient volume and the majority of German travelers on the Red Sea coast. Six locations distribute response across the central tourist corridor, from the northern hotel belt through to the main promenade, with one site equipped for laboratory and imaging support.

## Key points

- **Six locations across the corridor.** Coverage spans the north hotel belt, central Sheraton Road, and the main tourist promenade.
- **Tourist Promenade as main outpatient site.** The two promenade clinics carry the highest walk-in volume and full outpatient capability.
- **Al-Kawther as diagnostic anchor.** Laboratory and X-ray support is routed through Al-Kawther for cases requiring imaging.
- **Hotel-branch presence at scale.** Pharaoh Azure, Minamark, and Sheraton branches sit inside the hotel footprint for immediate access.
- **Dispatch hub for mobile delivery.** The cluster also functions as the dispatch point for hotel-room and mobile-unit deployment across the city.

## What this means for ADAC

Hurghada is where the bulk of cases originate and where response times are shortest. The six-site footprint targets walk-in coverage during clinic hours and dispatch coverage out of hours, so that the travel distance between traveler and first medical contact stays short regardless of where in the city they are staying.
