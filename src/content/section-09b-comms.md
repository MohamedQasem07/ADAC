## Context

Communication runs through a small number of well-defined channels so nothing is lost between intake and closure. A single named case coordinator is responsible for every traveler from first contact to final report. Status updates are pushed at defined milestones rather than only on request.

## Key points

- **Named coordinator.** One coordinator owns the case for the full lifecycle, with direct phone and email on file at ADAC.
- **Email as system of record.** Case notifications, medical reports, package-coded invoices, and escalation summaries all run through email and land in the case file.
- **Phone hotline.** Reserved for urgent escalation, live clinical handoffs, and any time-critical decision that cannot wait for asynchronous reply.
- **Assistance portal.** Optional secure portal for case dashboards, status lookups, and historical case data.
- **Language coverage.** Clinical communications in English by default, German on request, with the working language confirmed at intake.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC always knows who to call, where the case sits, and which channel to use. Status flows through the same channels every time, which removes the variability that usually shows up in cross-border assistance work and makes case auditing straightforward.
