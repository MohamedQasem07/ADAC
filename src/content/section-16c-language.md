## Context

Language in a clinical setting is a quality-of-care issue, not a hospitality detail. Travelers describe symptoms more precisely in a language they are comfortable in, and physicians document more precisely when they fully understand what they are being told. The HMC language model is built around this — English as the operational default, German where staffing allows, with Arabic and Russian available for local logistics.

## Key points

- **English by default.** Clinical conversations, written documentation, and discharge instructions default to English across the network.
- **German-speaking physicians where available.** German-language clinical support is offered on request, particularly for hotel-room visits, subject to staffing on the day.
- **Operations Department covers four languages.** The case-coordination layer operates in English, German, Arabic, and Russian — covering booking, transport, and administrative logistics.
- **Medical reports in English.** The Detailed Medical Report is issued in English as standard, with German on request for family-physician handover.
- **Written safety-net advice in preferred language.** Post-discharge advice is given in writing in the AG Holder's preferred language where staffing allows.

## What this means for AG Holders

The traveler is heard accurately, documented accurately, and discharged with paperwork their family doctor at home can read directly. Language stops being a source of clinical risk and becomes a routine operational detail.
