## Context

The catalogue is priced against the clinical work that is actually performed. Rates are neither padded with hidden contingencies nor undercut to win volume. The intent is to set a single number per package that absorbs normal variability, so neither side has to renegotiate at the case level.

## Key points

- **One flat rate per package.** Each of the 65 packages carries a single price, set so that small clinical variability sits inside the rate.
- **Mode of delivery is inside the rate.** Clinic, hotel-room, or mobile delivery is the same price for the same package.
- **Time of day is inside the rate.** Daytime and after-hours delivery do not change the package price.
- **Paediatric and adult priced independently.** Where workload genuinely differs, the catalogue carries separate codes rather than a single blended number.
- **No hidden contingencies.** Rates are not loaded against escalation risk; escalation is handled and quoted separately under §13.3.

## What this means for ADAC

Pricing is predictable at the point of authorization rather than at the point of invoice. ADAC can read the catalogue once and know what a given case will cost, without having to negotiate around delivery mode, time of day, or routine clinical variability on a case-by-case basis.
