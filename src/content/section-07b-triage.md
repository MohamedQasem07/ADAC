## Context

Triage is the clinical decision that drives everything downstream. An HMC physician — not an administrator — reviews the notification, asks targeted clinical questions and classifies the case using a standard green / yellow / red severity scale. The same step picks the tentative category from the 9-category catalogue, which in turn frames the candidate package. Triage runs in parallel with intake to avoid wasting the first hour.

## Key points

- **Physician-led, not script-led.** A doctor or trained triage nurse makes the call, working from clinical signs rather than a generic decision tree.
- **Three-band severity scale.** Green for simple outpatient, yellow for outpatient with labs / imaging / observation, red for escalation criteria triggering ER pathway and immediate ADAC notification.
- **Category code set early.** The tentative category from the 9-category framework is fixed at triage, which narrows the package shortlist before dispatch.
- **Mode-of-service decision included.** Clinic, hotel room or mobile unit is chosen at triage from clinical fit, traveler mobility and geography.
- **Pediatric and senior protocols.** Age-specific thresholds are applied where indicated rather than handled as exceptions.

## What this means for ADAC

Clinical triage at the front of the journey is what makes the rest of the workflow defensible. By the end of this step ADAC has a severity, a category and a delivery mode — enough to confirm cover and predict the package band. It is also the gate that catches red cases early and routes them to escalation before time is lost.
