## Context

Once the severity band is set, the case is routed to the right HMC site and the right delivery mode. Routing combines clinical needs with travel logistics — the AG Holder's hotel, mobility, and the equipment required for the case. A named case coordinator is assigned at this step and stays with the case until closure.

## Key points

- **Clinic visit.** Preferred for trauma with imaging, cases needing in-house diagnostics, or multi-hour observation under nursing supervision.
- **Hotel-room visit.** Preferred when the traveler is unwell but stable, the case is green or yellow, and portable equipment is sufficient.
- **Mobile clinic unit.** Used for extended cases, resort-side multi-traveler situations, or when a hotel room cannot host the required setup.
- **Nearest equipped site.** Distance and traffic are factored into dispatch — the nearest site with the right equipment and language coverage wins.
- **Named coordinator.** One coordinator is assigned per case for the full lifecycle, with direct ADAC contact details on file.

## What this means for ADAC

ADAC always knows who owns the case and where the traveler is being treated. The three delivery modes carry the same flat-rate package price, so routing choices are made on clinical and logistical grounds rather than on margin, and ADAC has a single point of contact from intake to closure.
